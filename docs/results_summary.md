# Results Summary

This pilot study evaluated LLM-mediated domain learning in Requirements Engineering.

## Corrected descriptive results

- K0 average: 31.82%
- K1 average: 93.47%
- Learning gain: +61.65 percentage points
- Condition A: 93.18%
- Condition B: 93.75%
- Difference between B and A: +0.57 percentage points

## Interpretation

The results suggest that LLM-generated instructional materials supported initial domain learning.

Condition B obtained a small numerical advantage over Condition A. However, the difference is too small to support a conclusive claim that personality-aware prompting outperformed the standard structured prompt.

The study should therefore be interpreted as a pilot focused on domain learning and protocol validation, not as a definitive validation of personality-aware prompting.
