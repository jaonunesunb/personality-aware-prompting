# Instruments Summary

The study used one pre-test and two post-test instruments.

## K0 pre-test

K0 measured initial familiarity with the public works monitoring domain.

It covered:

- distinction between planning and execution;
- purpose of measurement documents;
- purpose of monitoring reports;
- evidence;
- responsibilities;
- delays;
- validation rules;
- basic interpretation of business rules in public works monitoring.

Maximum score: 44 points.

Corrected average:

- 14.00/44
- 31.82%

## K1 post-tests

K1 was applied after exposure to the LLM-generated instructional materials.

There were two K1 versions:

- Measurement Bulletin;
- Construction Monitoring Report.

Each version had 11 knowledge questions scored from 0 to 2.

Scoring rubric:

- 0: incorrect, absent, or outside the domain;
- 1: partially correct, generic, or containing relevant imprecision;
- 2: correct and adequate.

Each K1 version had a maximum of 22 points.

Each participant completed two K1 tests, for a total maximum of 44 points.

Corrected average:

- 41.13/44
- 93.47%

## Measurement Bulletin K1

The Measurement Bulletin version evaluated whether participants understood:

- the purpose of the Measurement Bulletin;
- the distinction between measurement and planning;
- the relation with the Physical-Financial Schedule;
- the relation with the Analytical Budget Spreadsheet;
- contract linkage;
- measured quantities;
- evidence;
- validations;
- glosa;
- ambiguities requiring specialist validation.

## Construction Monitoring Report K1

The Construction Monitoring Report version evaluated whether participants understood:

- the purpose of the report;
- the distinction between monitoring report, Measurement Bulletin, and Daily Construction Report;
- managerial and technical consolidation;
- supporting documents;
- planned-versus-executed comparison;
- delays;
- risks;
- pending issues;
- evidence;
- ambiguities requiring specialist validation.

## Confidence item

Each K1 version included one self-confidence item from 1 to 5.

This item was analyzed separately and was not included in the knowledge score.
